How I work

Start with the user, not the feature

Before writing a user story, I want to understand who it's for, what they're trying to do, and what's in their way. Discovery first — always.

Make trade-offs visible

Prioritization shouldn't be a black box. I use RICE, value/effort, and MoSCoW — and make every trade-off explicit to stakeholders before committing.

Treat engineering as a partner

The best product decisions I've made came from conversations with engineers who said "there's a smarter way." Technical feasibility is a product constraint.

Outcome over output

Shipped features are inputs. What matters is whether they moved the metric. I track post-launch performance obsessively — and I'm comfortable killing features that didn't work.

Write specs people want to read

Clear acceptance criteria, explicit edge cases, annotated wireframes. A vague spec is a tax on engineering velocity.
