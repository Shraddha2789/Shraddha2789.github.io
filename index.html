At Iksula, I learned to ship fast and let data change my mind. At Deloitte, I learned that stakeholder alignment is product work, not admin overhead. At Signzy, I learned that B2B products live or die on client relationship depth ‚Äî and that compliance constraints can become competitive moats. At Sapiens, I'm learning that enterprise roadmap prioritization is its own discipline: making trade-offs explicit, in writing, before anyone disagrees.

The thread through all of it: I care deeply about understanding why a product decision matters before deciding what to build.

Start with the user, not the feature

Before writing a user story, I want to understand who it's for, what they're trying to do, and what's in their way. Discovery first ‚Äî always.

Make trade-offs visible

Prioritization shouldn't be a black box. I use RICE, value/effort, and MoSCoW ‚Äî and make every trade-off explicit to stakeholders before committing.

Treat engineering as a partner

The best product decisions I've made came from conversations with engineers who said "there's a smarter way." Technical feasibility is a product constraint.

Outcome over output

Shipped features are inputs. What matters is whether they moved the metric. I track post-launch performance obsessively ‚Äî and I'm comfortable killing features that didn't work.

Write specs people want to read

Clear acceptance criteria, explicit edge cases, annotated wireframes. A vague spec is a tax on engineering velocity.
